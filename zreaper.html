Wheatfield with a Reaper


Van Gogh painted this walled field from his hospital room. The reaper labours in the heat of the sun.

The wheat,painted with thick gobs of yellow, undulates around him. For Van Gogh, wheat was a symbol of the eternal cycle of nature and the transience of life. He saw the reaper as 'the image of death . . . in this sense that humanity would be the wheat being reaped.
