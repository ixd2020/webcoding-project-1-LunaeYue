Shoes


Worn-out shoes were an unusual choice of subject for a painting. An acquaintance of Van Gogh's in Paris described how he bought old work shoes at a flea market.

Then he walked through the mud in them. Van Gogh made a number of still lifes of this subject. He painted Shoes over another picture: a view from his brother Theo's apartment.

Van Gogh used the same canvas more than once on other occasions too. It was a way of saving money.
